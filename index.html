Divinity in Humanity

H.H. Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi

Sahaja Yoga was founded by H. H. Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi, a renowned spiritual guru born on March 21, 1923 at exactly twelve o’clock on the day of the spring equinox in a town in Chindwara the geographical center of India.

H. H. Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi discovered this process of Kundalini Awakening called Self-Realisation on 5th May 1970 and has since given Self-Realisation to seekers all around the world.

H. H. Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi said that while in Nargol, on 5 May 1970, she witnessed the rising of the Primordial Kundalini within Herself. Later she described the experience as follows: "I saw my Kundalini rising very fast like a telescope opening out and it was a beautiful color that you see when the iron is heated up, a red rose color, but extremely cooling and soothing". She stated that the potential for all humanity to gain spiritual self-awareness was realised at this time, which H. H. Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi characterised as a "historical process of en-masse self-realisation and inner transformation". Soon after She founded Sahaja Yoga to provide seekers their Self Realisation.

Self Realization

During the meditation you need to keep your left hand with palm upwards on the lap and to place the right hand on various parts on the left side of your body.

Please follow the steps as shown and repeat each affirmation from your heart. You are referring to your Kundalini Energy (Mother).


Step 1: Place your right hand on the heart and in a relaxed way, ask three times:

"Mother, Am I the Spirit?"

Step 2: Then place the hand at the height of the stomach and ask three times:

"Mother, Am I my own Guru?"

Step 3: With the right hand in the lower part of the abdomen, pressing a little on the left groin, repeat six times:

"Mother, please give me the pure knowledge"

Step 4: Put your hand back to your abdomen and make the following statement ten times:

"Mother, I am my own Guru"

Step 5: Raise the right hand to the heart and with confidence, affirms twelve times:

"Mother, I am the Spirit"

Step 6: With the hand on the left side of the neck, turn your head to the right and affirm sixteen times:

"Mother, I am not guilty of anything"

Step 7: Now, place your hand on your forehead and repeat several times:

"Mother, I forgive everyone and I forgive myself"

Step 8: Next, place your hand on the back of the head and repeat several times:

"Mother, if I have made an error against my Spirit, consciously or unconsciously, please forgive me"

Step 9: Keeping our eyes closed, we place the hand on the head, moving the scalp in a clockwise direction and asking with humility:

"Mother, please give me my Self Realization"

Benefits of Sahajayoga

How Does Sahajayoga Help Manage Stress?

Sahajayoga works on multiple levels to combat stress

Physiological Relaxation

Sahajayoga lowers heart rate, blood pressure, and anxiety, promoting a deep state of relaxation by activating the parasympathetic nervous system.

Energy System Balance

Sahajayoga meditation aims to clear blockages in energy channels (nadis), believed to improve well-being by promoting balanced energy flow and reducing stress.

Deep Relaxation

Sahajayoga meditation induces a profound state of relaxation, allowing the body and mind to release tension and accumulated stress.

Emotional Balance

By harmonizing the energy centers in the body, Sahajayoga helps regulate emotions and promotes a sense of inner peace and contentment.

Mindfulness and Self-Awareness

Sahajayoga meditation helps you become more aware of yourself, allowing you to identify stress triggers and respond calmly instead of reacting impulsively.

Clarity of Mind

Through thoughtless awareness, practitioners experience a quieting of the mind chatter, enabling clearer thinking and enhanced decision-making abilities.

Improved Resilience

Regular practice of Sahajayoga strengthens the nervous system and cultivates resilience to stressors, enabling individuals to navigate life’s challenges with greater ease.

Enhanced Well-being

Sahajayoga meditation promotes holistic well-being by fostering harmony between mind, body, and spirit, leading to greater vitality and overall health.
